# data-burst

A submodule of the biosemiotic-refractor repository, destined for a brainstorming where I will refine and create the ideas before the definitive place within the main branch.

## 1. friction points

Here are some of the friction points that i will likely need to address as the framework evolves:

The Model Energy Consumption Paradox: I noticed that maintaining cognitive dissonance and computing complex nuance costs a lot of energy. Yet, current machine learning systems (which would presumably run this "refractor") are already consuming massive amounts of planetary energy. How do we build a computationally expensive "tension-holder" that doesn't physically damage the "planetary holobiont" through massive data-center power consumption?

The Privilege of Nuance: I state: "When a human secures its environment, when it has a surplus of energy and safety... it aims for 'more'." This is true (echoing Maslow's hierarchy of needs). However, billions of humans do not have a surplus of energy and safety. Binary thinking is currently ravaging the world precisely because so many people feel economically or physically threatened; A framework for nuance requires a baseline of safety that much of the world currently lacks.

How is it implemented? Epistemologically first, of course, nut practically, what is it? Is it an algorithm? A new way to train neural networks (e.g., changing the loss function so models are rewarded for exploring rather than resolving)? Or is it more purely a sociological manifesto? Another protocol? At inference or training level? 

How do we cure the manifactured parasocial-biological panic inside our algorithms and ourselves?

We can start from the very basis, brecognizing that the rush to certainty is a primitive reflex, the fast thinking.

### 1.1 solutions

I will not offer rigid "solutions" to these three friction point, that would be a hypocritical rush toward cognitive closure.

I will pass these concepts through the Biosemiotic Refractor itself and the other modules and submodules of the ecosystem, refracting them into pathways and perspectives for me to hold in my "tension-holder-nerve" as the repository grows.
